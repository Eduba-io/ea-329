A read on where AI actually fits in TravelMize's stack

Every travel-tech team in 2026 is answering the same question from prospects and investors: what is your AI story. The honest answer is usually that parts of the product genuinely need a language model, most parts do not, and nobody has drawn the line clearly.

That line is the work.

Eduba runs a frame we call 60/30/10. Roughly 60% of most products is traditional code and database work. 30% is rule-based logic that a language model handles worse than a decision tree. 10% is actually an AI problem, and that 10% is where you should be spending model budget, evaluation time, and engineering attention. Teams who skip this distinction end up with GPT in the search bar, a chatbot that cannot close a booking, and a product story that collapses in due diligence.

60%
Traditional code
Booking engine, supplier inventory, reconciliation logic. Postgres work.
30%
Rule-based logic
Pricing rules, policy enforcement, itinerary templating. Decision trees beat LLMs here.
10%
Actually AI
Freeform client preferences, long-context itinerary assembly, supplier communication parsing.

Applied to a travel-tech product like TravelMize, the 60/30/10 read usually looks like this. The booking engine, the supplier inventory database, and the reconciliation logic belong in the 60. Pricing rules, policy enforcement, and itinerary templating belong in the 30. Freeform client preferences, long-context itinerary assembly, and supplier communication parsing belong in the 10. The result of drawing the line is a product roadmap that a technical buyer believes and a demo that holds up when someone pokes at it.

The nearest precedent

The closest match in our case library is Simple Lyfe Stays, a 46-property short-term rental operator. Same shape of problem: multi-unit, ops-heavy, small team, customers running parts of the workflow in email. We packaged the work as a scoped sprint where the operator chose depth over breadth. The deliverable was an orchestration layer and a playbook the team could run without us. That is the right template for TravelMize if the booth conversation confirms the hypothesis.
